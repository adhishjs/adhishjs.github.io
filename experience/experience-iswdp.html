Overview

Semiconductor Workforce Development

The India Semiconductor Workforce Development Program (ISWDP) is a comprehensive initiative tailored to bridge the process technology and device design skill gap in the rapidly expanding semiconductor sector[reference:0]. It is a collaboration between the Indian Institute of Science (IISc), Samsung Semiconductor India Research (SSIR), and Synopsys Inc[reference:1].

As a trainee in Cohort 4, I completed all three levels of the program — Level 1, Level 2, and Advanced — gaining hands-on experience in semiconductor device physics, TCAD simulations, and VLSI design flows. I was recognized as a Top Performer in both Level 2 and the Advanced level, and received the Samsung Fellowship.

  • Performed semiconductor process and device simulations using the Synopsys Sentaurus TCAD suite for CMOS process and device modeling.
  • Received hands-on training in semiconductor device physics, CMOS design, fabrication processes, and VLSI architecture.
  • Progressed through three levels of increasing complexity, from fundamentals to advanced device characterization.

Curriculum Structure

Three-Level Progression

The program is structured in three levels, each building on the previous one to develop a comprehensive understanding of semiconductor device design and TCAD simulation[reference:2].

Level 1Text Books to TCAD — Semiconductor physics fundamentals, basic device structures, and doping profiles
Level 2Advanced TCAD — Process and device simulation, CMOS modeling, and characterization
Advanced LevelPrecision characterization techniques, RF testing, and emerging technology evaluation
Tools UsedSynopsys Sentaurus TCAD · Structure Editor · Sentaurus Visual · Workbench

Key Learnings

Semiconductor Device Physics

Gained a deep understanding of semiconductor physics, including carrier transport, doping, junction behavior, and the fundamental principles underlying modern CMOS devices.

TCAD Process & Device Simulation

Learned to use Synopsys Sentaurus TCAD for process and device simulation — from creating basic structures to modeling complex CMOS devices with accurate doping profiles.

VLSI Design & Fabrication

Received comprehensive training in CMOS design, fabrication processes, and VLSI architecture, bridging the gap between theoretical knowledge and practical semiconductor manufacturing.

Characterization & Testing

Explored precision characterization techniques, measurement methodologies, and RF testing essential for evaluating and qualifying semiconductor materials and devices.
