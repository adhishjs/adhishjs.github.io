Overview

This project implements a 2 mm × 2 mm ASIC in SCL 180nm that realizes a digital Leaky Integrate-and-Fire (LIF) neuron — the fundamental computational unit of Spiking Neural Networks (SNNs). The chip receives a 16‑bit digital input representing the summed contribution of presynaptic spikes weighted by their corresponding synaptic weights, and processes it through a fully synchronous digital pipeline.

Designed for neuromorphic computing and edge AI, the LIF neuron performs temporal integration, programmable leakage, threshold comparison, spike generation, and refractory reset — all in real time at 100 MHz. The IC is taped out and currently awaiting post‑silicon validation.

How it works

  • 1 Integration — membrane potential update

    The 16‑bit digital input (sum of weighted presynaptic spikes) is added to the neuron's previous membrane potential, which is stored and fed back through a register. This feedback retains memory of past activity, enabling temporal processing across clock cycles.

  • 2 Leakage — programmable decay

    A right‑shift operation is applied to the stored membrane potential, scaling it down by a fixed ratio that corresponds to the leakage constant. The result is passed through a 2's complement circuit to generate its negative equivalent, which is fed back to the adder to subtract the leaked portion — mimicking biological charge decay.

  • 3 Threshold comparison & spike generation

    The resulting membrane potential is continuously compared with a preset 16‑bit threshold. When the membrane potential equals or exceeds the threshold, the neuron generates a digital spike output (binary event).

  • 4 Reset & refractory period

    Following spike generation, the reset logic discharges the membrane potential to a resting value (typically zero), ensuring the neuron exhibits a refractory period before the next spike can occur — a key biological property.

  • 5 Synchronous control & output

    All functional units (integrator, right‑shift leakage, 2's complement subtractor, comparator, reset logic) are coordinated by a central clock and control logic. The IC outputs an 8‑bit membrane potential and a binary spike, ready to be cascaded with other neuron ICs for large‑scale SNN architectures.
