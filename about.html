Bio

I'm Adhish JS, a B.Tech student in Electronics and Communication Engineering at NIT Puducherry, passionate about designing intelligent hardware that bridges the gap between research and real-world applications. My primary interests lie in Analog & Mixed-Signal IC Design, Digital ASIC Design, Neuromorphic Computing, and Embedded Systems, where I enjoy transforming ideas into efficient hardware solutions.

My journey into chip design began with embedded systems and FPGA development, where I discovered the excitement of building hardware from the ground up. Over time, that curiosity evolved into designing custom integrated circuits, leading me to work on two ASIC tapeouts, contribute to published research, and gain hands-on experience with the complete custom IC design flow — from circuit design and simulation to layout, verification, and post-layout analysis. These experiences have strengthened my interest in understanding of transistor-level circuit design while reinforcing the importance of system-level thinking.

Beyond ASIC design, I enjoy working across the hardware stack, including FPGA acceleration, PCB design, and embedded systems. I believe that a strong engineer should be comfortable moving between different levels of abstraction, whether designing analog circuits in Cadence Virtuoso, implementing digital architectures on FPGAs, or developing hardware-software solutions for edge AI applications.

I enjoy solving challenging engineering problems, continuously learning new technologies, and collaborating on projects that create meaningful impact. My long-term goal is to contribute to the development of high-performance, low-power semiconductor technologies while advancing research in analog, mixed-signal, and AI-focused hardware design.
